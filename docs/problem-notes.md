# Problem Notes

Early notes on three possible problem areas — the user groups affected and how they currently cope. These feed Lab 2 opportunity scanning. (Maps to the GitHub Issue "Identify three possible problem areas".)

---

## Problem Area 1: Finding apartments near campus is difficult  *(front-runner)*

- **Who feels it:** Students seeking off-campus housing, especially new and international students.
- **How serious / how often:** Every intake and lease cycle; high stress for newcomers.
- **Current alternatives and why they fall short:** Facebook groups, agents, and word of mouth — scattered, with unclear prices and distances.
- **Evidence to gather in Lab 2:** Ask students how they found their current place and what was hardest.

## Problem Area 2: IT students get stuck without timely help

- **Who feels it:** IT and computer science students.
- **How serious / how often:** Weekly, especially during assignments and exams.
- **Current alternatives and why they fall short:** Stack Overflow, generic AI tools, and classmates — not tailored to their course or focused on learning.
- **Evidence to gather in Lab 2:** Ask IT students where they get stuck and how they currently find help.

## Problem Area 3: Buying and selling used items among students is messy

- **Who feels it:** Students moving in or out each semester.
- **How serious / how often:** Every semester start and end.
- **Current alternatives and why they fall short:** Facebook/LINE buy-sell groups and notice boards — posts get buried, no search, no trust.
- **Evidence to gather in Lab 2:** Ask students what they buy/sell and how they do it now.

---

**Early lean:** Problem Area 1 (Student Apartment Finder) is our front-runner; the other two stay open for Lab 2 screening.
